A Charge to Preach

"I charge thee therefore before God, and the Lord Jesus Christ, who shall judge the quick and the dead at his appearing and his kingdom; Preach the word; be instant in season, out of season; reprove, rebuke, exhort with all longsuffering and doctrine." (2 Timothy 4:1-2)

1. A _____________ Charge – vs. 1

Definition: "charge" - a Greek word diamarturomai which means to testify earnestly, religiously or to solemnly affirm

A. Preach the Word

"Whereunto I am appointed a preacher, and an apostle, and a teacher of the Gentiles." (2 Timothy 1:11)

B. Be Ready

Definition: “be instant” – to stand by, be present; to be at hand

C. Reprove

Definition: “reprove” – Greek word elegcho meaning to convict, refute, confute

D. Rebuke

Definition: “rebuke” – Greek word epitimao meaning to tax with fault, chide, censure severely to admonish or charge sharply

Quote: “The task of the preacher is to comfort the afflicted and afflict the comfortable.” – Vance Havner

E. Exhort

Definition: “exhort” – Greek word parakaleo which literally means “to call to one’s side,” “call for,” summon

3. A _____________ Charge – vs. 2b

A. Longsuffering

Definition: “longsuffering” – Greek word makrothumia which means patience, endurance, constancy, steadfastness, perseverance; slowness in avenging wrongs

Quote: "Patience is the grace of the man who could revenge himself, but chooses not to.” – John Chrysostom 

"Not given to wine, no striker, not greedy of filthy lucre; but patient, not a brawler, not covetous;" (1 Timothy 3:3)

B. Doctrine

Definition: sound – means free from any error 

Definition: doctrine – means teaching, instruction, that which is taught, teachings, precepts.

"Having a form of godliness, but denying the power thereof: from such turn away. For of this sort are they which creep into houses, and lead captive silly women laden with sins, led away with divers lusts, Ever learning, and never able to come to the knowledge of the truth." (2 Timothy 3:5-7)
